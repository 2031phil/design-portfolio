Defining the Mission

What is Token4Change?

The first step in this project was to identify what key values the brand identity I was later going to create would need to reflect. So, what is this brand about?

This startup wants to give the youth of Liechtenstein a place to participate in decisions concerning societal and environmental issues by offering them an innovative digital platform.

Through a bottom-up organisation they want to empower young people to become self-led individuals and increase their interest in politics.

Brand Sprint

To understand the client’s ideas about their brand even better I conducted a brand sprint with them. Within an hour we were able to take all of the different ideas they had about their brand and crystallize them down into the following categories: Vision for the future, What, How & Why of the brand, values, target audiences and competition.

In the first exercise I asked the client to envision what their brand will look like in 5, 10, 15 and 20 years. The results of this ranged from building a solid national network to handing the project over to the next generation.

Next, they needed to think about the what, how and why of their brand. This showed me that they were very clear about the “what” and the “why”, but not so much on the “how” yet.

The top 3 values and beliefs they identified easily. They described their brand with the terms: youth-led, fun & intrinsic motivation.

The team was also very clear on their target audiences; 12 to 18 year old teenagers (at first only in their home country of Liechtenstein) and other youth organizations as well as some governmental organizations.

Lastly, we talked about competition in the market. The client identified some competitors, such as “AHA+” and “Jugend:Digital”, but also stated that there was a large gap in the market that was not addressed so far.

Personas

Persona 1

Tim is a 15-year-old who lives in a small town. He has a deep love for nature and enjoys spending time outdoors. He also likes staying informed by watching the news. However, he often feels frustrated by how little his community does to protect the environment, and he worries about the effects of climate change. One of the things that bothers him most is the amount of litter in his favorite park. He wishes people would take better care of the places he loves, but he feels powerless to make a difference. Since he can’t take part in decision-making processes, he struggles to find a way to stand up for his beliefs.

Persona 2

Lisa is a 26-year-old who works for a youth organization in a small town. She is passionate about working with teenagers and is dedicated to helping them become more aware of the world around them and think for themselves. However, she faces challenges in making a bigger impact—her organization has a limited budget, which makes it difficult to carry out larger projects. On top of that, only a few people in the region know about the organization and its mission. Despite these obstacles, Lisa remains determined to inspire and support the youth in any way she can.

Rationale

The coin is an essential part of the logo, not only symbolizing the “Token” in Token4Change, but also the act of working together & helping each other to make the world a better place.

I had the idea from the start to use a literal depiction of a token in my logo, but it took some fiddling around to make it work together seamlessly with the text. I ultimately chose the font “Onest” because the brand wanted to appear modern while also maintaining a bit of playfulness, which comes through in certain letters of this font. Lastly, I added more depth to the logo through some shading to make it more appealing to a younger audience and to give it a more tangible feel.

Digital platform

Background

From the beginning on it was clear for the Token4Change team that they wanted to build a digital platform, likely in the form of an app, that would need to attract the youth and encourage them to engage in difficult topics like politics and today’s problems.

After creating a brand design that communicates and reflects Token4Change’s mission and values, the next step was to apply that design to an actual product that makes sense.

In this section I will share with you how I went about designing such a platform, targeted towards the youth of Liechtenstein, with user experience in mind.

However, it is important to mention that my project only involved the creation of a small prototype as a sort of proof of concept.

User Problems

The Liechtenstein youth:

  • doesn’t know how to engage in democracy
  • might not want to participate because it sounds like work
  • may not be interested in politics at all
  • is not aware of democratic values and why democracy is worth defending

Solutions

  • Educate them about democracy through engaging content
  • Provide participation opportunities for them
  • Encourage them to make a change and initiate their own actions
  • Reward them with tokens that can be traded in for fun, real-life prizes

Project Takeaways

Simplicity is Key

Small organizations often have limited resources, so branding and app design must be clear, intuitive, and easy to maintain. On top of that, with the client’s target audience in mind, it is important not to overwhelm teens with information.

Effective Visual Symbolism

Using meaningful symbols (e.g., tokens for participation) can help convey the brand’s mission clearly, even more effectively to teenagers, who might not be interested in such a project at first.

Branding Alone Won’t Solve Participation Issues

A strong brand and app can help, but real engagement requires ongoing outreach, incentives, and real-world action opportunities.
