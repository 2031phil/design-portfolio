Personas

Persona 1

Tim is 18 years old and wants to visit his friend in the hospital. He is very skilled with technology and is a supporter of more digitalisation in everyday life. He has never planned a visit to a hospital before and would rather use an app to do it than to call or write an email.

Persona 2

Marie is a 54 year old woman who wants to make an appointment with a specialist at the hospital. She is not that good with technology and can get lost in more complex UIs. She also doesn't have the best orientation in big places such as hospitals and doesn't know if she'll be able to find the right room.

Core Design Idea and USP

Practicality

  • Introducing the way to make appointments in the 21st century
  • Plan visits quickly and efficiently
  • Always find your destination on a visit
  • All the important contact infos in one place, quickly accessible

Simplicity

  • Better organisation decreases the time people spend searching and planning visits or contacting the right people in emergencies
  • Easy navigation to and within hospital makes visits less difficult, less stressful and more convenient
  • Simple layout and modern design enabling usability for all ages

Project Takeaways

Working with boxes

While sometimes it may look good or even be necessary for a clear structure, I learned that there is no need to box everything in. After having initial wireframes with all of the content inside boxes, I revised some of the screens to look more open by taking some elements (like titles) out of the boxes, which improved the look and feel dramatically in my opinion.

Getting the spacing right

Working with a lot of differently sized elements like texts and icons can be tricky, especially when putting them all together on a wireframe. It is thus important to arrange them very delicately, paying close attention to the spacing between every element in relation to their size, their importance and their context, even more so when designing for mobile.

First ever project

This was my first ever UX Design project when I was a first semester at SRH Berlin, so please don't be too harsh on it : ). It was a lot of fun to work on and I learned more than I could summarize in three points.
