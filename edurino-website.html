Persona & User Journey

Persona

In order to create a design that is user friendly, it is important to first understand the user. Thus, I started this project by creating my persona, Lilli.

Lilli is a 36 year old, married mother who works full-time, as does her husband. She has a five year old son who will soon start pre-school. Lilli and her husband have always been very focused on their careers, but lately realized that they should focus more on the development of their son, who spends a lot of time on their iPad playing games instead of preparing for starting school and learning important skills.

Project Takeaways

Creating a persona to get a more detailed understanding of your target group

This the first project where I really took the time to create a detailed persona and a user journey for that persona. Even though it wasn't the most fun thing to do, I did find that it helped me at different stages in the design process to remind me of how this product should look and feel.

Designing for different screen sizes before starting to code.

I hadn't coded a lot of websites before this project and was still learning. Up until then I had always first designed, then coded and then figured out the responsiveness through experimenting in the code editor. This project taught to me to first design the site, then think about how it'll look on different screen sizes and only then start to code.

Interactivity can be key

Adding interactive elements to a website can make all the difference when a user opens it for the first time and has to decide if he'll stay or leave. Catching the user's attention and making his stay enjoyable can be the key to getting a successful conversion.
